Hitch
Smarter student travel

What makes Hitch different

Hitch is built for students who commute every day. Instead of paying for expensive taxis or waiting on unreliable public transport, users can find lifts with other students already travelling the same route.

Social

Travel with people your own age, in your college or even your course. It’s an easy way to meet new people.

Cost

Only €15 per month. Much cheaper than taxis and more flexible than public transport.

Convenience

No waiting, no switching buses. Just book, hop in, and go.

Target Audience

Hitch is aimed at college students aged 18–30, including both undergraduate and postgraduate students. These are people who travel daily, often during peak times, and want a quicker and cheaper way to commute.

The app is designed to be simple, with fast sign-up and easy booking. It also adds a social element, allowing students to travel with others in the same college or course.

Market Size & Growth

The global carpooling market was valued at around $8.6 billion in 2024 and continues to grow due to rising fuel costs and traffic congestion.

Even within one university like DCU, reaching just 10–20% of students could result in 2,000–4,000 users. As commuting costs increase, demand for services like Hitch is expected to grow.

Competitor Analysis

Hitch competes with apps like BlaBlaCar, Carma, Bolt, and Uber, as well as public transport.

What makes Hitch different is its focus on students. It’s cheaper than taxis, faster than buses, and offers a more social and flexible experience.

Unlike traditional services, Hitch allows students to arrange both regular and one-off lifts based on their changing schedules.

Market Entry Barriers

Developing an app requires investment, so Hitch will begin with a Minimum Viable Product focusing only on essential features.

Building trust is another challenge. To address this, the app will include email verification, user ratings, and profile checks.

Competition is strong, but Hitch focuses on a specific niche — students — and plans to partner with colleges to grow its user base.
